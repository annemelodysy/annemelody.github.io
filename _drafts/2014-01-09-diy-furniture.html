When I set my mind on something, I do everything I can to get it. I am like a spoiled kid who just can't sleep until she gets that toy she's been eyeing on for days. The only difference is that I do it myself instead of asking other people to get it. The only times I allow other people to get it for me is when they offer it.

Like when I was a kid, I loved collecting Polly Pockets. Obviously I couldn't afford them then so my parents gave me a challenge. They said that they would buy me a Polly Pocket every time I get a place in the top 3 honor roll in class. Fortunately, this was in the 3rd grade when I got transferred to another section (instead of being in the 1st section). I studied so hard that I was able to achieve the goal. I got my Polly Pockets and was a fairly happy kid.

Years later, I had this urge to try living on my own. I felt like this was something I should try before turning 30. I guess you would say I'm that sort of person who likes to experience things as much as she can.

So when I decided it was time for me to move out and live in an apartment, I had my mind set to it completely. First, I had to consider the budget, then the place, then reconsider the budget again, and the cycle goes on.

I finally found a bare apartment I could customize and that also fits the budget. Now for some makeover. Unfortunately, I am super picky so it's kind of hard to get something that I want without having to compromise a little. Kind of like when I go shopping and I spend an entire day at the mall and find just 1 or 2 things that I like. It might just be me or it has something to do with the very little available choices in Cebu.

The studio unit with its default not-so-nice-looking vinyl floor. By the way, the walls were beige so I decided to repaint it white to make the room look bigger. Light colors especially white give extra space to your narrow room.

Furniture Time! It's hard to find a good furniture store in Cebu (I'm sure there's a few out there but not exactly my taste) so I decided to go the other route. Instead of buying furniture, I went with custom-made.

After several visits to known furniture shops, I found a small store in Parkmall(now in Ayala new wing) that caught my fancy. Projekt specialized in small apartments and they seemed to have better taste than most shops in Cebu. When I learned that they also customize furniture, I was ecstatic. The search was finally over.

Taking a leap and trusting on my instinct, I sent them some pictures of the furniture I liked as reference since I had a hard time describing exactly what I wanted. Luckily, they were able to make it look similar.

Some Tips for the Do-it-Yourselfer:

  • Custom-made furniture can sometimes be cheaper than commercialized ones. Considering good quality, in my experience it's easier to resell custom-made ones.
  • You don't have to spend too much to get a good item. You'd be surprised to find some super cheap yet decent things in White Gold such as this lovely stool.
  • Another good find in White Gold. Pretentious, no?
  • Containers and baskets are very handy for organizing things. Nothing beats sleeping in a well-organized apartment.

Baskets bought in Rustan's. Also try checking Muji for more baskets like these.

Here's another idea for storing shoes. I bought these really cheap shoe storage in Ikea. They are stackable and very lightweight. I even place my wet umbrella in one of the shelves.

  • Take Advantage of promotions and sales. I bought most of my other things like the mattress, pillows, sheets, vacuum, induction cooker during SM's big sale. I think I only paid 50%-70% of the actual price!
  • Lastly, take good care of your furniture. I was able to get back most of what I spent on my furniture because they looked brand new even after several months.

Side note: spend on experiences not just things. I kept my apartment minimal and bought only things I needed because the fun part was the experience of living in one.
