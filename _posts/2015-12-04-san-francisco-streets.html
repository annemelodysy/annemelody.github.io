It's been eight months since we moved into this city. It doesn't feel that long since we unpacked our luggage and called this tiny apartment our new home. But the light summer clothes that hung in our closet when we first arrived has now turned into cozy sweaters and thick coats. It's cold outside. I've never been a "cold weather" type of person. I don't get excited by boots and scarves or snow (thankfully, there's no snow in San Francisco) and I get cold pretty easily. But I'm slowly adjusting. Besides, it gives me a wonderful excuse to drink all the hot chocolate I want. Hah!

These past few months, we've been saving up for our long-overdue honeymoon so we've decided to hold off on some adventures. San Francisco is expensive so we thought of ways to entertain ourselves without spending too much. One way to achieve that is to just wander around the city and taking in all the things that make this city beautiful and unique.
