I wanted to see for myself what the hype was about so I did some research and a couple of floating markets got my attention. ### Damnoen Saduak Floating Market Known for its vibrant display, it attracts tourists from around the world. The urge to take photos almost drove me to pick this market, but I got discouraged by the idea of packed tourists. I don't *hate* them. People should spend their money and travel however they want (granted they don't hurt others). But the problem is that it usually is too crowded and things cost more in places with a lot of tourists. ### Amphawa Floating Market It's not as colorful as Damnoen Saduak, but the locals love it for its delicious food. I decided this was the place for me. As much as I wanted to take beautiful photographs, I thought it would be a change to make my stomach happy this time. I didn't even realize that I spent my reserves until I left. My favorite was the freshly-made pad thai (I don't have a picture, too hungry to bother) and the tasty coconut balls in banana leaves. The milk tea, as usual, was perfect for the hot and humid afternoon!
