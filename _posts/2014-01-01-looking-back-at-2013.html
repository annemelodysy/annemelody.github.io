I’m practicing my memory and checking to see how the previous year went for me. So much has happened in the span of one year and I feel like I’ve learned so many things as the months went by. Here’s a summary of my 2013.

January

First Sinulog Out in a Long Time

The last time I went to Sinulog was when I was probably less than 10 years old. I’ve lost interest in the parades ever since it turned into a chaotic celebration. But that particular January, Jeremie and I had the same thought. We had nothing to do so we decided to brave the waves of people and went to Baseline. BAD IDEA. It was chaotic and I felt like a lemon about to get squished out of its juices.

February

10-day Trip to Ilocos Norte

The biggest highlight of the year was probably our trip to Ilocos Norte. It was filled with discovery and relaxation. The trip showed us that the Philippines is truly gifted with Natural Resources and Picturesque Scenery.

We trekked Mount Pinatubo and met some new friends along the way; We explored the well-preserved streets of Vigan; We went Sand boarding in Paoay, Laoag; We were humbled by the giant Bangui Windmills; and we were pampered in a gorgeous resort in Pagudpud. We stayed in Kapuluan Resort during Valentines Day. The minimalist-inspired resort also served organic food (yes, we saw their vegetation).

March

Went Berserk and Decided to Go Pixie

After several debates in my head, I finally decided to go Pixie. It was a long stretch but totally worth it. The new hair gave me confidence.

Camping in Zambales

As if the 10-day trip in Ilocos wasn’t enough! My family and I went to Zambales last March. We walked around the streets of Olongapo, went camping in Anawangin, explored the nearby islands, went to Zoobic, and on our last day, dropped by Ocean Park Manila. This was my gift to my 2 younger brothers who had graduated that month.

April

Turned 6 Years with Jeremie

After all the “wanderlusting”, I didn’t realize we had already turned 6 years together. Anyway, he was out-of-town during our Anniversary so I didn’t really expect much…until a surprise came knocking.

A big bouquet of flowers! Now, we both don’t see the significance in giving flowers but this one was an exception.

Started Living on my Own

After searching for the right home to live in, I finally found an empty apartment in Mabolo that was just perfect for me. It was love at first sight! I couldn’t wait to transform it. There was a point though when I couldn’t stop buying things I didn’t need but thankfully I was able to overcome that impulsive behavior.

May

Opened Happy Sacks

On May 11, we opened our first stall in JY Square. The whole process of running a business was new to me and it was very stressful at first. I learned so many things from Happy Sacks like how to pick your staff, how to interview and train them, how to manage and run the numbers, how to deal with people in a non-introvert way, how to promote products through marketing, how to solve problems, how to create a system, and so so much more. It gave me nightmares (literally), but it was so much fun at the same time.

June

Experienced the Untouched beauty of Siquijor

I don’t know what it is about me but I can’t seem to say no to traveling. Sure I still have lots to do for our new business, but when the opportunity of travel came, I had no doubts about it. Siquijor felt like being lost in a secret island that only a handful of people have experienced. We spent some time sun-bathing and swimming and floating at Jeremie’s family’s beach lot.

Watched Dolphins in the wild

We were about to give up when they didn’t show up the last 30minutes or so but then at the very last minute, when everyone was saying we should just go back home, they came out! Dolphins are pranksters like that.

July

Learned to Make my Own Mochi

July was uneventful for me but I learned a new skill. I had then been too addicted to Mochi and couldn’t stop craving for the little balls so I decided to do some research and replicate the recipe. It was a partial success! We got the texture and taste, however, I couldn’t find the right way to place the ice cream without them melting quickly.

August

Swam with Sea Turtles

It was incredibly mind-blowing! We were like a foot away from them! We were not allowed to touch them though but just looking at them blows you away. This was my first time seeing them in the wild and not on television. On a side note, Apo island is great too!

September

Jeselle’s Homecoming

One of my closest childhood friend came home from the US. We rarely see her so this was something big. We had plans to go out of the country or out of town but everyone got too busy and it’s really hard to plan something when there’s a whole bunch of people involved so we ended up swimming at Lex Hotel. We ate dinner at my apartment and we had a slumber party after.

Featured in Sunstar

Happy Sacks got featured in a prominent newspaper in Cebu! Jeremie and I were so nervous during the interview.

October

Jeremie Moved to Singapore

Other good news, Jeremie got an offer in Paypal so he had to move there. I was happy and just a little sad that we were going to be separated by water. But things went ok. So this is how long-distance relationships look like. I expected something far worse but it turned out okay.

Folk Fiction’s first Pop-up Event

Happy Garaje with their handmade toys, Ballista Games with their unique board games, and Happy Sacks with our dumplings and milktea teamed up to create Folk Fiction.

November

Ra Ra Riot, Explosions in the Sky

We celebrated Jeremie’s birthday at the Camp Symmetry Festival in Singapore. Lots of great bands played but we were there for Ra Ra Riot and Explosions in the Sky.

The World’s 2nd Largest Aquarium

Intrigued, we decided to check it out. We both love the ocean and sea creatures! All I can say is…Wow. That was a big aquarium. I can’t wait to see the world’s largest aquarium.

Experienced National Geographic’s 50 Greatest Photographs Exhibit

Enough said. National Geographic is just…aggghhh

Attended a Writer’s Festival

Learning and getting inspired at the same time!

Sad to Say Goodbye to Happy Sacks

We sold the company to a good friend of mine. I am sure they will take good care of it. It’s sad to say goodbye to your first startup but on the plus side, at least it lives on.

December

Birthday Surprise

Another one of his crazy surprises! I really didn’t know he was already here and I had embarrassingly told everyone he was coming on the 15th because it was my birthday. Anyway, I cried that night when the blindfolds dropped and I saw him standing with balloons. Such a sweetheart.
